# Prompting Basics

Effective prompting is the key to getting the most out of AI, whether you're creating apps in Playlab or using them. Good prompts help you externalize your thinking and communicate clearly with AI systems.

<Info>
  **What is a Prompt?**
  
  A prompt is the input you give to an AI to guide its response. Think of it as a request or instruction that helps the AI understand what you want it to do or generate.
</Info>

    When using Playlab apps, how you phrase your requests significantly impacts the quality of responses you receive.

    <Accordion title="Externalizing Your Needs">
      Good prompts help you:
      - Clarify what you're actually looking for
      - Break down complex questions into manageable parts
      - Provide necessary context that might not be obvious
    </Accordion>

    <Accordion title="Effective User Prompting">
      <Accordion title="Be Specific and Clear">
        - **Poor**: "Help with my essay"
        - **Better**: "I'm writing an essay comparing renewable energy sources. Can you help me outline three key differences between solar and wind power?"
        
        Being specific helps the AI understand exactly what you need, saving time and producing more relevant responses.
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Provide Context">
        - **Poor**: "What's the answer to question 5?"
        - **Better**: "I'm working on a 7th-grade geometry worksheet about calculating the area of irregular shapes. For question 5, I need to find the area of this composite figure [description]. Here's my approach so far..."
        
        Context helps the AI understand your situation and provide appropriate guidance.
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Ask Follow-up Questions">
        - If the initial response isn't quite what you need, refine your request
        - Build on previous interactions: "That's helpful, but could you explain more about..."
        - Specify what aspect you want expanded: "Can you elaborate on the third point about energy storage?"
      </Accordion>
    </Accordion>

## <Icon icon="circle-question" size="24" className="inline-block align-text-bottom" /> Frequently Asked Questions

<Accordion title="How long should my prompts be?">
  Effective prompts can be short or long depending on the task. For simple tasks, a concise prompt may be sufficient. For complex tasks requiring specific formatting or thinking patterns, longer prompts with examples and clear instructions are often better.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What if my Playlab app isn't responding the way I expected?">
  If your app isn't producing the expected responses, try these Playlab-specific strategies:
  
  1. **Review your base prompt**: Check your main app instructions for clarity and specificity
  2. **Test with the references toggle**: Turn references on and off to see if they're helping or hindering
  3. **Examine starter inputs**: Ensure they're guiding users to provide the right information
  4. **Add explicit guardrails**: Include instructions about what the app should NOT do
  5. **Use the test console**: Playlab's testing environment lets you try different inputs and refine your prompts
  6. **Check for conflicting instructions**: Make sure different parts of your prompt don't contradict each other
  
  Remember that in Playlab, your prompt is the foundation of your entire application, so spending time refining it is worth the investment.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I use the same prompting techniques for different AI tools?">
  While the fundamental principles of clear communication apply across different AI systems, each may have specific strengths, limitations, and preferred formats. What works well in Playlab might need adjustments for other AI tools.
</Accordion>
