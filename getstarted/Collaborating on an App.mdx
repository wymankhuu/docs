V2 separates app collaboration from workspace membership. To let a peer help build an app, you add them as an **Editor** on that app. Editor access is per-app, so the peer gets to edit the one app you shared and nothing else.

## What changed from V1

In V1, collaboration on an app worked through workspace membership. To give a peer edit access to your app, you added them to the workspace as a **Creator**. That granted edit access to your app along with every other app in the workspace.

V2 keeps the workspace concept and moves collaboration onto each app. The Share modal on any app lets you add specific people with specific permissions. Workspace membership is still useful for activity grouping, but it is no longer how you grant edit access.

## How to add an Editor

<Steps>
 <Step title="Open the app">
 Go to the app you want to collaborate on. The app builder or the app overview page both work.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Click Share">
 The **Share** button is in the top right of the app view.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Add the peer's email or name">
 Type their email address or, if they are in your organization, start typing their name. The matching members appear as you type.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Set the permission to Editor">
 Pick **Editor** from the permission dropdown. This is the only permission level that grants editing rights.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Send">
 Click **Send**. The peer gets a notification and can open the app from their dashboard.
 </Step>
</Steps>

## Editor permissions

An Editor on your app can do the following:

- Edit the app's instructions and prompt
- Add, remove, and reorder references
- Change starter inputs and welcome message
- Adjust model selection and conversation settings
- Test the app and view its activity

An Editor cannot:

- Reassign ownership
- Delete the app
- Change who else has access
- Publish the app

Publishing stays with the owner. If your Editor is ready to ship a new version, the owner clicks **Publish**.

## When to use Editor vs Viewer

Add a peer as **Editor** when you want them to help build, refine, or maintain the app.

Add a peer as **Viewer** when you want them to use the app or see its activity without changing it. Viewers see the app on their dashboard once it is published. They cannot edit anything.

For sharing with people who only need to use the app, see [Sharing with individuals](https://learn.playlab.ai/features/Sharing%20with%20Individuals).

## Removing or changing access

Open the app, click **Share**, find the person in the recipient list, and either change their permission or remove them. Changes take effect immediately.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
 <Accordion title="Can I have more than one Editor on an app?">
 Yes. Add as many Editors as you need. Each one has the same edit rights.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="Do Editors see other apps in my workspace?">
 No. Editor access is per-app. Adding someone as an Editor on App A does not give them any access to your other apps.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="Does this replace workspace-level sharing?">
 For collaboration on a specific app, yes. Workspace membership still controls workspace-level activity views and access to apps that are added to the workspace as a whole.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="What if my peer is in a different organization?">
 That works the same way. Type their email in the recipient field. They get an invite if they are not already in Playlab. Cross-org Editor access is supported in V2.
 </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if my Editor leaves the org?">
    Their access is removed automatically when they're removed from the org. The app stays with you.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I see what changes my Editor made?">
    App version history shows what changed and who made the change. Open the app and look for the Versions tab.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can two Editors edit the same app at the same time?">
    Yes, but the last save wins on the same field. For close collaboration, agree on who owns which section before editing together.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
