<Note>
  In V2, you usually don't need to remix. To use an app in another class, [add it to the workspace](/features/Cross-Workspace%20App%20Use) — activity stays segmented per class and updates flow automatically. Remixing is for when you want to substantially customize an app for one context.
</Note>

Remixing creates a personal copy of an existing Playlab app. You can modify the copy independently of the original. In V1, remixing was the primary way to deploy or share an app. In V2, sharing and cross-workspace use cover most of that pattern, and remixing is reserved for cases where you want a separate, independent version.

## How to remix

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find an app you want to remix">
    Browse the [Playlab Community](https://playlab.ai/explore) or any app you have access to. You can remix from the published version's page or from an app card in your workspace (click the **⋮** menu).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Remix and name your copy">
    By default the new name is `[REMIX] Original App Name`. Choose which workspace to save it to. Click **Remix** to generate the copy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize">
    You land in the builder for your new app. Edit the prompt, references, starter inputs, and settings. The original app is unaffected.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## When to remix instead of cross-workspace use

Most cases that called for remixing in V1 are better served by [adding the app to your workspace](/features/Cross-Workspace%20App%20Use) in V2. Remixing still makes sense in three situations:

**Heavy customization.** If you want to substantially change the prompt, references, or starter inputs for one class, remix. The original app stays clean.

**Frozen-in-time pedagogy.** If you want a class to use the app exactly as it was at a point in time, even if the owner ships updates, remix. Your copy is independent.

**Learning the prompt.** Students learning to build apps often remix as a way of seeing how an app is built. The remix is for teaching more than for production deployment.

For most other cases, adding the app to the workspace is the simpler path.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What is the difference between remixing and creating a new app?">
    Remixing starts with a complete working template you can customize. Creating a new app starts with an empty canvas. Remix when you want to build on someone else's work; create new when you want to start from scratch.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will my remixed app change the original app?">
    No. Remixing creates an independent copy. Changes to your remix do not affect the original, and vice versa.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I remix an app that was already remixed by someone else?">
    Yes. You can remix any app you have access to, including remixes of remixes.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I need permission to remix someone's app?">
    For public apps in the Playlab Gallery, no — making an app public implies consent to remix. For private apps shared with you, the owner controls whether remixing is allowed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I make my remixed app private?">
    Yes. New apps in V2 — including remixes — start private. You decide when to share or publish.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will updates to the original app affect my remix?">
    No. Once you remix, your version is independent. If you want updates to flow automatically, use [cross-workspace app use](/features/Cross-Workspace%20App%20Use) instead of remixing.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
