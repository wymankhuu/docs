Org admins and teachers invite people into Playlab through three paths: org-level invitation, workspace email invitations, or workspace invite links. Each fits a different situation.

<Tip>
 Quick reference: download the [Adding Members One Pager](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1LkOE6fcNHm9ZVFb63gwMsOCN68o44guASGWQdSyM970/view).
</Tip>

## Method 1: Org-level invitation

This is the path for adding people to the organization itself, before placing them in any specific workspace.

<Steps>
 <Step title="Go to the org Members tab">
 From the org dashboard, click Members. You see the list of current org members and the Add Members and Create Invite Link buttons.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Click Add Members or Create Invite Link">
 **Add Members** sends direct email invitations. **Create Invite Link** generates a shareable link.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Set the permission">
 Pick the role you want new members to have at the org level. Most members start as Members; coaches or co-admins might be Admins.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Send or share">
 For Add Members, paste the email list and click Add Members. For Create Invite Link, copy the link and share through email or your communication channel.
 </Step>
</Steps>

## Method 2: Workspace email invitation

This is the path for teachers adding students or co-teachers to a specific workspace. The workflow is documented in detail in [Adding members to a workspace](https://learn.playlab.ai/getstarted/Adding%20Members%20to%20your%20Workspace).

A short version:

<Steps>
 <Step title="Open the workspace">
 Go to the workspace where you want to add members.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Click Add Members">
 The Members tab has the same Add Members button.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Add emails and set roles">
 Paste the list, pick a role, send.
 </Step>
</Steps>

If the email belongs to someone who is not yet a Playlab user, they receive an invite to create an account and land in the workspace once signed up.

## Method 3: Workspace invite link

This is the path for class rosters or programs where you want self-service signup.

<Steps>
 <Step title="Open the workspace and go to Members">
 Same starting point as Method 2.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Click Create Invite Link">
 Pick the role new members should have when they join.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Copy and share">
 Paste the link into your class platform, email it to families, or share it through Slack.
 </Step>
</Steps>

You can revoke an invite link at any time. Revoking stops new joiners; people who already used the link keep their access.

## Choosing the right method

| Situation | Method |
| --- | --- |
| Adding a co-admin to the org | Org-level invitation, role Admin |
| Adding a Learning Partner to the org | Org-level invitation, role Member or Viewer |
| Adding a class roster | Workspace email invitation if you have the list, otherwise invite link |
| Adding a co-teacher to a workspace | Workspace email invitation, role Manager |
| Running an after-school club | Workspace invite link |
| Adding a guest observer | Workspace email invitation, role Viewer |

## Best practices

Things admins and teachers have asked about:

Use direct email invitations when you have a roster from a student information system. The list import is faster than relying on students to follow a link.

Use invite links for fluid attendance: clubs, summer programs, professional development sessions.

Set the default permission low. It is easier to upgrade a Member to a Manager than to walk back excessive access.

Document who has Admin access to your org. Periodic review (twice a year) catches stale Admin assignments from past staff.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
 <Accordion title="What if I don't have a list of students yet?">
 Use Create Invite Link. Set the permission to Member and share the link. Students join as they sign up.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="Can students invite other students?">
 Only if their workspace permissions include "share beyond workspace" and your org allows it. By default, students cannot invite others. See [Workspace building permissions](https://learn.playlab.ai/getstarted/Workspace%20Building%20Permissions).
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="How do roles cascade between org and workspace?">
 Org-level Admin status grants visibility into every workspace. Workspace Manager status applies only inside that workspace. The two are independent: a Workspace Manager is not automatically an Org Admin.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="What if a student leaves my org?">
 Remove them from the org Members tab. They lose access to all workspaces. Their conversation history is preserved for admin review.
 </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
