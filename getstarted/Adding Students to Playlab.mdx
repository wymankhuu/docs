<Info>
  **Key points**

  - Two paths: **org-level invitation** (whole organization) or **workspace invitation** (a specific class or team)
  - Each path supports both direct email invites and shareable invite links
  - Roles assigned at invitation determine what new members can do
</Info>

Org admins and teachers invite people into Playlab through two paths: an **org-level invitation** that adds someone to the organization itself, or a **workspace invitation** that places someone in a specific class or team. Pick the path that matches what you're trying to do.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Go to the org Members tab" icon="users">
        From the org dashboard, click **Members**. You see current org members along with the **Add Members** and **Create Invite Link** buttons.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Click Add Members or Create Invite Link" icon="user-plus">
        **Add Members** sends direct email invitations. **Create Invite Link** generates a shareable link anyone can use to join.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Set the permission" icon="user-check">
        Pick the role new members should have at the org level. Most start as Members; coaches or co-admins might be Admins.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Send or share" icon="send">
        For **Add Members**, paste the email list and click **Add Members**. For **Create Invite Link**, copy the link and share it through email or your communication channel.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    For org-level role assignments, see [Org permissions and roles](https://learn.playlab.ai/features/Org%20Permissions%20and%20Roles).

## Choosing the right method

| Situation | Method |
| --- | --- |
| Adding a co-admin to the org | Org-level invitation, role Admin |
| Adding a Learning Partner to the org | Org-level invitation, role Member or Viewer |
| Adding a class roster | Workspace invitation, email or invite link |
| Adding a co-teacher to a workspace | Workspace invitation, role Manager |
| Running an after-school club | Workspace invitation, invite link |
| Adding a guest observer | Workspace invitation, role Viewer |

## Best practices

Things admins and teachers have asked about:

**Invite at the org level** when someone needs access that spans workspaces — co-admins, Learning Partners, or members who'll be assigned to multiple classes later.

**Invite at the workspace level** when someone only needs access to a specific class or team. It keeps activity and visibility scoped to that one workspace.

Set the default permission low. It is easier to upgrade a Member to a Manager than to walk back excessive access.

Document who has Admin access to your org. A twice-a-year review catches stale Admin assignments from past staff.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What if I don't have a list of recipients yet?">
    Use **Create Invite Link**. Set the permission to Member and share the link. People join as they sign up.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can members invite other members?">
    Only if their workspace permissions include "share apps outside the workspace" and your org allows it. By default, members cannot invite others. See [Workspace building permissions](https://learn.playlab.ai/getstarted/Workspace%20Building%20Permissions).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do roles cascade between org and workspace?">
    Org-level Admin status grants visibility into every workspace. Workspace Manager status applies only inside that workspace. The two are independent — a Workspace Manager is not automatically an Org Admin.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if a member leaves my org?">
    Remove them from the org Members tab. They lose access to all workspaces. Their conversation history is preserved for admin review.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if a user forgets their password?">
    For SSO orgs, users reset through their identity provider (Google, Microsoft, Clever). For email accounts, the sign-in screen has a Forgot Password link.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can a user belong to multiple orgs at once?">
    Yes. A Playlab account can be invited into more than one organization. Each org controls what they see when they're inside it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a maximum number of users per org?">
    Partner orgs have generous limits. Most schools and districts don't hit them. If you do, your Learning Partner can request more.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
