Workspaces hold members and apps. Adding members is a two-path flow in V2: direct invitation by email, or a shareable invite link.

<Note>
 **Permissions matter**

 Roles you assign on invitation control what members can do in the workspace. Choose carefully. You can change roles later from the Members tab.
</Note>

## Two ways to add members

V2 supports both direct invites and invite links. Pick the path that matches your situation.

**Direct invite (Add Members).** You type or paste a list of emails, set the permission, and Playlab sends each one an invite. Best when you have a known list and want a record of who you invited.

**Invite link (Create Invite Link).** Playlab generates a link you can share through email, Slack, or a class document. Anyone who clicks the link joins the workspace at the permission level you set. Best for class rosters where you want students to self-serve.

## Method 1: Add Members directly

<Steps>
 <Step title="Open the workspace and go to Members">
 The Members tab lists current members and shows the Add Members and Create Invite Link buttons.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Click Add Members">
 A dialog opens with an email input field and a permission selector.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Add emails">
 Type or paste a list of emails. Playlab parses comma-separated and newline-separated lists.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Pick the permission">
 Choose **Member** for most people, or **Manager** for co-teachers and admin support.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Click Add Members">
 Each invitee gets an email. They click through and join the workspace.
 </Step>
</Steps>

## Method 2: Create an invite link

<Steps>
 <Step title="Open the workspace and go to Members">
 Same Members tab as for direct invitation.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Click Create Invite Link">
 A dialog opens with the permission selector and a Copy Link button.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Pick the permission for the link">
 Anyone who joins through the link gets this permission.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Click Create & Copy Link">
 The link is generated and copied to your clipboard.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Share the link">
 Send it through email, Slack, or paste it into a class document. Each person who clicks it joins the workspace.
 </Step>
</Steps>

You can revoke an invite link at any time from the Members tab. Revoking does not affect people who have already joined; it only stops new joiners from using the link.

## Workspace roles

The roles you can assign to members are:

**Manager.** Can invite members, change permissions, add apps, view all activity, and change workspace settings. Multiple managers per workspace are allowed.

**Member.** The default role. Can use apps in the workspace and (depending on building permissions) may be able to build apps. See [Workspace building permissions](https://learn.playlab.ai/getstarted/Workspace%20Building%20Permissions).

**Viewer.** Read-only access. Useful for instructional coaches or partners who need to see what's happening without participating.

For org-level roles that span workspaces, see [Org permissions and roles](https://learn.playlab.ai/features/Org%20Permissions%20and%20Roles).

## Best practices

Things teachers have asked about:

Use Add Members when you have a class roster from a student information system. The list import is faster than expecting students to follow a link.

Use Create Invite Link for after-school or club settings where attendance is fluid. The link makes self-service possible.

Set the default permission for the link to Member. If a colleague needs Manager access, invite them directly with the right role.

Revisit roles each semester. Coaches who were active last year may not need access this year.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
 <Accordion title="What if I add an email that doesn't have a Playlab account?">
 The invite still goes out. The recipient creates an account when they click the invite, then lands in the workspace.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="Can I add the same person to multiple workspaces?">
 Yes. Each workspace tracks its own membership. The same person can be a Member in one workspace and a Manager in another.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="How do I remove a member?">
 Open the Members tab, find the person, and click Remove. They lose access immediately. Their conversation history stays in the workspace's activity log.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="Can I see who joined through an invite link?">
 Yes. The Members tab shows how each person joined: direct invite or link.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="What if I revoke a link by accident?">
 Generate a new one. The new link works the same way. People who joined through the old link keep their access.
 </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
