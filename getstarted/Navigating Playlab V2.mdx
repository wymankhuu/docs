V2 reorganizes the platform around four clear containers. The change gives you direct control over your apps, sharper boundaries between classes, and new ways to share what you build with the people you build for. If you used V1, most of what you knew still works. The structure around it has shifted to give you more room to move.

<Note>
  Read this first if you're new to V2. The rest of the V2 docs assume you know the containers below.
</Note>

## The four containers

V2 has four containers. Everything you do happens inside one of them.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Organization" icon="building" iconType="lucide">
    Your school, district, or partner. Holds workspaces, members, collections, and policies. Most people belong to one organization.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Workspace" icon="folder-tree" iconType="lucide">
    A class, team, or group inside the org. Workspaces hold members and apps, with their own activity and permissions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="App" icon="square-pen" iconType="lucide">
    A Playlab app. Lives in a workspace. Can be added to other workspaces and to collections without remixing.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Collection" icon="layers" iconType="lucide">
    A curated set of apps you can share like a single resource. Useful for partner deployments and starter packs.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What's new for you

The container structure is the means. The ends are new things you can do that V1 made hard.

**Your apps are yours.** New apps start private. You and your org's admins can see them. Nobody else, until you share or publish. Drafts stop leaking into a workspace before you're ready.

**Sharing is direct.** Add a peer as Editor or Viewer on one app. They get access to that app, not your whole workspace. You can change or revoke access at any time.

**One app, many classes.** Add the same app to as many workspaces as you need. Each class sees segmented activity. Update the app once, and every class using it sees the new version.

**Collections for curated bundles.** If you ship a set of apps to partners or districts, build a Collection once and share it. Recipients see updates as you publish them.

**Workspace controls you set.** Toggles on each workspace decide whether members can build apps, see each other's work, or share apps beyond the workspace. The settings can match the class.

**Activity at the level you need.** Workspace, member, or org. Same data, different scope. Teachers see their class. Admins see across classes.

## How content moves

V2 lets you share an app, add it to another workspace, or include it in a collection. Duplicating to share is no longer required.

<Steps>
  <Step title="An app is owned in one place">
    The person who creates the app owns it. The app lives in a single workspace.
  </Step>
  <Step title="The owner shares it">
    Sharing happens through the **Share** modal: with individuals, groups, organizations, or as a Collection.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Recipients add it where they need it">
    A teacher can add a shared app to any of their workspaces. A partner can drop a Collection into their org and use it immediately.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Updates flow automatically">
    When the owner publishes a new version, everyone with access sees the update. No re-sharing required.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  This replaces the V1 pattern of remixing an app per class. See [Using an app across multiple classes](https://learn.playlab.ai/features/Cross-Workspace%20App%20Use) for the educator workflow.
</Tip>

## How V1 maps to V2

Most V1 concepts have a V2 home. A few moved.

| V1 concept | V2 home | What changed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Organization | Organization | Same. More visibility for admins. |
| Workspace | Workspace | Same container. Now supports granular building permissions. |
| App owner = workspace creator | App owner = the person who built it | Apps have direct owners. Editors are added per-app. |
| Remix-per-class | Add the same app to multiple workspaces | One app, many classrooms, segmented activity. |
| Workspace-creator collaboration | Editor sharing on the app | Collaboration is per-app. Workspace membership grants different things. |
| Public sharing only | Public, org, group, or individual sharing | Sharing is granular and reversible. |
| Activity per workspace | Activity per workspace, plus member detail | Org admins see flags and activity centrally. |
| (no equivalent) | Collections | Curated sets of apps for partner-style deployment. |

## Where things moved

Three reorganizations are worth flagging up front.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Sharing is now its own surface" icon="share" iconType="lucide">
    In V1, you shared apps by adding people to a workspace. In V2, every app has a **Share** button that opens a modal where you can share with people, groups, or whole organizations. Workspace membership is still useful for activity grouping. It is no longer how you grant access to a single app.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Activity lives at every level" icon="trending-up" iconType="lucide">
    In V1, activity was workspace-only. In V2, you can review activity inside a workspace, drill into a specific member, or see flags across an entire organization. Each level surfaces a different question.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Collections replace remixing for deployment" icon="layers" iconType="lucide">
    In V1, a partner who wanted to share a curated set of apps with five districts had to remix each one into each district's workspace. In V2, the partner builds a Collection once, shares it with the districts, and updates flow automatically.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What this means for you

If you build apps, your apps are private until you share them, and Editor sharing replaces workspace-level collaboration. See [V2 for Builders](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V2%20for%20Builders).

If you teach, your workspace gives you new visibility into student activity and finer control over what students can do. See [V2 for Educators](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V2%20for%20Educators).

If you admin an org, you have a centralized view of activity and flags across all workspaces. See [V2 for Admins](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V2%20for%20Admins).

If you deploy across a network, Collections were designed for you. See [V2 for Networks or Districts](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V2%20for%20Networks%20or%20Districts).

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why did the structure change?">
    V1 worked well for individual builders and small teams. As partners scaled to dozens of orgs and thousands of students, three patterns showed strain: workspaces were doing too many jobs (membership, sharing, activity, ownership), remixing made deployment fragile, and admins had no central place to see what was happening. V2 splits those jobs into purpose-built containers.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will my apps still work?">
    Yes. Apps move into V2 with their owners and members preserved. Activity history is retained. Some labels and entry points changed. The apps themselves are unchanged.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Where is X now?">
    If you are looking for a specific V1 feature or page, check the [V1 → V2 cheat sheet](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V1%20to%20V2%20Cheat%20Sheet). It maps every common workflow side-by-side.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is V2 only available to certain orgs?">
    V2 is the default for new organizations as of May 2026. Existing organizations migrate on a rolling schedule with their Learning Partner. See [Migrating from V1 to V2](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/Migrating%20from%20V1%20to%20V2).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
