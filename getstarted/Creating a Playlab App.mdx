Creating a Playlab app is the start of using AI in your work. This guide walks through the build flow. The mechanics work the same in V2 as in V1, with one small change: you can start a new app from either the org or a workspace.

<Note>
 **Plan before you build**

 Take a few minutes to think about what problem you want your app to solve. A clear purpose makes the build faster and the result more useful.
</Note>

## The app creation process

<Steps>
 <Step title="Start a new app">
 From your org dashboard or any workspace, click **New App**. V2 lets you start from either entry point.
 
 {/* IMG-20: App creation entry point */}
 <Frame>
 <img src="/images/v2_app_create_entry.png" alt="The V2 New App entry point reachable from both the org dashboard and a workspace." />
 </Frame>
 </Step>
 <Step title="Name your app and add a description">
 The name shows in your dashboard and in any workspace where the app is added. The description helps users understand what the app does. You can change both later.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Write the AI instructions">
 The instructions are the prompt that shapes how the app behaves. Tell the AI who it is talking to, what kind of help to give, and any boundaries to respect. Be specific.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Add references (optional)">
 References give the AI context: a curriculum document, a worked example, a rubric. The AI uses references to ground its responses. See [Adding references](https://learn.playlab.ai/getstarted/Adding%20References).
 </Step>
 <Step title="Test the app">
 Use the chat panel inside the builder to talk to your app as a user would. Refine the instructions based on what you see.
 </Step>
 <Step title="Publish or share">
 When you're ready, click **Publish** to make the app available, or click **Share** to add specific people. Both flows are described in [Publishing your app in V2](https://learn.playlab.ai/features/Publishing).
 </Step>
</Steps>

## What changed from V1

Two things:

**Entry point.** V1 required you to be inside a workspace to start a new app. V2 lets you start from the org dashboard too. The build experience after that is identical.

**Privacy.** V1 apps were visible to the workspace from the moment you created them. V2 apps start private. They become visible only when you share or publish. See [App privacy and visibility](https://learn.playlab.ai/features/App%20Privacy%20and%20Visibility).

## Best practices

Things that have worked for builders:

Plan a small scope for your first app. A focused tool that does one thing well beats a broad assistant that tries everything.

Test from the user's perspective. Open the conversation panel and ask the questions a real student or colleague would ask. The gap between what you wrote and how the AI behaves often shows up here.

Iterate in small loops. Make a small change to the prompt, test, repeat. Big rewrites are harder to attribute to a specific behavior change.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
 <Accordion title="Can I duplicate an app?">
 Remixing creates a copy you can modify independently. From any app's overview, click **Remix**. The copy is owned by you. The original is unaffected.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="How do I move an app between workspaces?">
 In V2, you do not move apps between workspaces. You add the app to additional workspaces. The app keeps a single owner and source. See [Cross-workspace app use](https://learn.playlab.ai/features/Cross-Workspace%20App%20Use).
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="Can I collaborate with a peer while building?">
 Yes. Add them as an Editor through the Share modal. See [Collaborating on an app](https://learn.playlab.ai/getstarted/Collaborating%20on%20an%20App).
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="What if I want to start over?">
 Delete the app from its overview page and start fresh. Or remix the app to keep the original around as a reference.
 </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a limit on how many apps I can create?">
    No fixed cap for accounts on a partner plan. Free-tier accounts have a soft cap that grows over time as you build.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I import an app from another platform?">
    Not directly. You can copy your prompt and references into a new Playlab app. We don't offer a one-click importer.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How do I save my work-in-progress?">
    Playlab saves automatically as you build. Drafts stay private to you and your org admin until you publish or share.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
