## At a glance

1. Workspaces still hold members and apps. The verbs you knew in V1 still work.
2. Workspaces can pull in apps from across the organization. Building an app inside the workspace is one option among several.
3. Workspace managers see all member activity, including for apps they did not build.
4. Building permissions are granular. You decide whether students can build, see each other's work, or share beyond the workspace.

## V1 parity for workspaces

If you ran a workspace in V1, the V2 version is recognizable. You still invite members, build or curate apps, and review activity. The same role structure carries over.

A few details changed:

- Workspace creation now includes a description field and building permissions on the form.
- Adding members is split into **Add Members** (direct invite by email) and **Create Invite Link** (a shareable link).
- The activity view filters by workspace, so a single app used in multiple classes shows segmented activity per class.

For the full walkthrough, see [Creating and managing a workspace in V2](https://learn.playlab.ai/getstarted/Creating%20Your%20Own%20Workspace).

## Curate apps as well as create them

In V1, a workspace held apps you created inside it. To use someone else's app, you remixed it.

V2 lets you add an existing app to a workspace without copying. The original owner keeps the app, you get to use it, and any updates the owner publishes flow through automatically.

## See all member activity in your workspace

Workspace managers in V2 see activity for every member, on every app in the workspace, even apps the manager did not build.

The new member detail page is the entry point. From the Members tab, click any member to see their full activity inside the workspace: which apps they used, the conversations they had, any flagged messages, and any apps they built.

This view is workspace-scoped. To see activity across workspaces, use the org-level dashboard (admin role). See [Workspace member detail](https://learn.playlab.ai/features/Workspace%20Member%20Detail) and [Reviewing student activity per class](https://learn.playlab.ai/getstarted/Reviewing%20Activity).

## Granular building permissions

In V1, workspaces had one shared mode. Either everyone could build or no one could. V2 splits this into three toggles set per workspace:

1. Whether members can build apps inside the workspace.
2. Whether members can see each other's apps and work.
3. Whether members can share their apps beyond the workspace.

You set defaults at workspace creation and change them later. Different classes can use the same workspace permissions or different ones, depending on the unit you are running.

For more, see [Workspace building permissions](https://learn.playlab.ai/getstarted/Workspace%20Building%20Permissions).

## What stays the same

Roles, invite flows, activity filtering, app templates, and the workspace home page all carry over from V1. Existing workspaces migrate with members and apps preserved.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
 <Accordion title="Will my V1 workspace migrate with its members and apps?">
 Yes. Existing workspaces, members, apps, and activity history move into V2. Your roles map to V2 roles automatically. See [Migrating from V1 to V2](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/Migrating%20from%20V1%20to%20V2).
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="Can I change building permissions partway through a class?">
 Yes. Permissions update from the workspace settings panel. The change applies going forward. Existing apps stay where they are.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="Do students see this member detail view?">
 No. Member detail is visible to workspace managers and org admins. Students see their own apps and conversations, the same as in V1.
 </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
