V2 reorganizes Playlab around four containers: organization, workspace, app, and collection. It changes how content travels between them. The building experience inside an app is the same. What sits around the app is different.

This page is the *why*. If you want a workflow-by-workflow comparison, see the [V1 → V2 cheat sheet](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V1%20to%20V2%20Cheat%20Sheet).

## What pushed the change

V1 worked well for individual builders and small teams. As partners scaled to dozens of organizations and thousands of students, three patterns showed strain.

**Workspaces were doing too many jobs.** Membership, sharing, ownership, and activity were all scoped to the workspace. Adding a peer to your workspace gave them edit access to every app in it. Sharing one app meant remixing it. Activity rolled up to the wrong place. The workspace became a bottleneck.

**Remixing made deployment fragile.** A partner who curated a set of apps for ten partner districts had to remix each app into each district's workspace. Updates required re-remixing. In practice, copies drifted within weeks. The pattern broke at any scale beyond a single classroom.

**Admins had no central place to see flags or activity.** A trust-and-safety incident in any workspace required walking every workspace to find the conversation. Cross-workspace patterns were invisible. The most-asked admin question, *what has this student been doing*, didn't have a direct answer.

V2 splits those jobs into purpose-built containers and adds a sharing surface that works across organizations.

## What V2 actually changes

Five capabilities are new in V2. Each links to its own guide.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Privacy by default" icon="lock" href="/features/App Privacy and Visibility">
    New apps start private. You and your org's admins can see them. Nobody else, until you share or publish.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Editor sharing on each app" icon="users" href="/getstarted/Collaborating on an App">
    Add a peer as Editor on one app. They get access to that app, not your whole workspace.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cross-workspace app use" icon="copy" href="/features/Cross-Workspace App Use">
    Add the same app to many classes without remixing. Activity stays segmented per class.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Collections" icon="layers" href="/features/collections/What are Collections">
    Bundle a curated set of apps and share the bundle. Updates flow to recipients automatically.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Centralized monitoring" icon="shield" href="/features/Reviewing App Activity">
    Org-level dashboards and a single flagged-messages list across every workspace.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What stays the same

The app builder, prompts, references, model selection, conversation flow, voice and dictation, and partnership integrations all carry over from V1. Existing apps move into V2 with their owners and members preserved. Activity history is retained. URLs you embedded in class materials redirect to V2 paths. Most of what your students do day-to-day is unchanged.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Is V2 just a rename of V1?">
    No. V2 reorganizes how content lives and travels. The building experience inside an app is the same. Sharing, deploying, and monitoring are different.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will V1 keep working?">
    V1 keeps working through the migration period. Specific sunset dates are announced through your Learning Partner.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How was V2 designed?">
    V2 was shaped by partner conversations over the past year. The Tiger Team training and rollout plan documents how the design pressures became product decisions.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
