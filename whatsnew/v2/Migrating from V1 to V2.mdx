## What's actually changing

V2 reorganizes how content and permissions work. The structure and workflow comparisons are documented in:

- [Navigating Playlab V2](https://learn.playlab.ai/getstarted/Navigating%20Playlab%20V2)
- [V1 → V2 cheat sheet](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V1%20to%20V2%20Cheat%20Sheet)

The short version: org> workspace> app> collection becomes the explicit hierarchy. Sharing happens through a Share modal on each app. Activity is visible at workspace, member, and org levels. Collections enable curated bundles for partner deployment.

## What stays the same

Migration preserves what was already working. Your apps, workspace members, conversation history, references, and integrations all carry forward.

| Thing | What happens during migration |
| --- | --- |
| Your apps | Move to V2 with the same prompts, references, and starter inputs |
| App owners | Preserved |
| Workspaces | Move to V2 with the same names, members, and apps |
| Workspace members | Preserved with mapped roles |
| Conversation history | Preserved and queryable |
| References (org, workspace, app level) | Preserved |
| Integrations (SSO, rostering) | Preserved |
| Activity history | Preserved |
| URLs and saved links | Old URLs continue to redirect to V2 paths |

You do not lose work in migration. The structure of how content lives changes; the content itself does not.

## Where things moved

A short reference for the most-asked relocations.

| In V1 you went to... | In V2 you go to... |
| --- | --- |
| Workspace> Members> Add Member | Workspace> Members> **Add Members** or **Create Invite Link** |
| Workspace> Apps> New App | Org or Workspace> **New App** |
| App> Publish> Pick visibility | App> **Share**> Publish toggle and visibility selector |
| Workspace> Activity | Workspace> Activity (workspace-segmented) or Org> Activity (cross-workspace) |
| Remix to deploy | App> **Share**> target org or **Add to Workspace** in another workspace |
| Curated bundles by remixing | Org> **Collections**> New Collection |

For the full mapping, see the [V1 → V2 cheat sheet](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V1%20to%20V2%20Cheat%20Sheet).

## What to do before migration

A short checklist:

- [ ] Note your active workspaces and the apps you care about
- [ ] If you have apps with shared collaborators, note who has edit access in V1
- [ ] If your org uses rostering, talk to your IT contact about sync timing
- [ ] Schedule a 30-minute review session for the day after migration
- [ ] Bookmark the [V1 → V2 cheat sheet](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V1%20to%20V2%20Cheat%20Sheet) for reference

If you have V1-specific URLs deeply embedded in class materials, those URLs continue to work. The redirects route them to the V2 equivalents.

## What happens during migration

Migration runs as a background process. The visible effects in your org:

- A short window (usually under an hour) where the org is read-only
- An announcement banner letting users know migration is in progress
- A welcome banner once migration completes, pointing to V2 introduction materials

Members do not need to log out and back in. The next page load after migration shows the V2 interface.

## What to do after migration

A short checklist:

- [ ] Open your dashboard and verify your workspaces and apps are present
- [ ] Try sharing an app from the new Share modal to confirm the flow
- [ ] Set workspace building permissions for any classes you run (these are off by default)
- [ ] Review members in your workspaces and adjust roles if needed
- [ ] Read [V2 for Builders](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V2%20for%20Builders), [V2 for Educators](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V2%20for%20Educators), or [V2 for Admins](https://learn.playlab.ai/whatsnew/v2/V2%20for%20Admins) depending on your role

If anything looks wrong, email [support@playlab.ai](mailto:support@playlab.ai). We can roll back individual issues.

## Help

For org admins on partner accounts, your Learning Partner is the first point of contact. They have a coordinated migration playbook and can walk you through the changes.

For self-serve users, [support@playlab.ai](mailto:support@playlab.ai) is the right starting point.

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
 <Accordion title="Can I roll back to V1 if something goes wrong?">
 Per-issue rollback is supported. We can revert specific apps, workspaces, or settings. Org-level rollback after migration is not supported.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="Will my links break?">
 V1 URLs continue to redirect to V2 paths. Bookmarks, saved links, and references in class materials should continue to work.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="Will my org admins change?">
 Roles map automatically. V1 Org Admins become V2 Admins. V1 Workspace Owners become V2 Managers in their workspaces.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="What if a student is in a class that hasn't migrated yet?">
 Each org migrates as a unit. If your org has migrated, all your workspaces are on V2. Cross-org sharing with a non-migrated org is supported during the transition window.
 </Accordion>
 <Accordion title="How long does migration take?">
 For most orgs, under an hour. Larger orgs with many workspaces and references may take longer. Your migration meeting is when this is scheduled.
 </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will my partners see anything different during my migration window?">
    Apps shared with them keep working. They see a small banner if they happen to open Playlab during the brief read-only window. No action required from them.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do I lose any data during migration?">
    No. Apps, conversations, references, members, and activity history all carry forward. URLs redirect.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I delay my migration date?">
    Yes. Migration windows are scheduled with your Learning Partner. If you need to postpone, reach out to them. New orgs default to V2 starting May 2026.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
