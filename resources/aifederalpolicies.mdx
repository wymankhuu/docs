## <Icon icon="landmark" size="24" className="inline-block align-text-bottom" /> Introduction

This resource summarizes the federal-level policies that shape how AI is used in U.S. K-12 schools. Federal action sits above the [state](/resources/aistatepolicies) and [district](/resources/aipolicies) frameworks, setting overarching priorities, grant criteria, and legal compliance obligations.

<Warning>
  Federal AI policy is evolving rapidly. Executive orders, agency guidance, and proposed rules may be updated, supplemented, or rescinded — always verify the current status directly with the issuing agency.
</Warning>

## <Icon icon="file-signature" size="24" className="inline-block align-text-bottom" /> Executive Order: Advancing AI Education for American Youth

**Signed:** April 23, 2025  
**Issuing Authority:** The White House

<div className="p-4 bg-blue-50 rounded-md border border-blue-200">
  <strong>Stated Policy Objectives:</strong>
  <ul className="mt-2">
    <li>Promote appropriate integration of AI into education</li>
    <li>Provide comprehensive AI training for educators</li>
    <li>Foster early exposure to AI concepts and technology</li>
    <li>Develop an AI-ready workforce and the next generation of AI innovators</li>
  </ul>
</div>

**Key Components:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="White House Task Force on AI Education">
    Established to coordinate federal AI-in-education efforts across agencies, plan the Presidential AI Challenge, and develop public-private partnerships.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Presidential AI Challenge">
    Within 90 days of the order, the Task Force was directed to establish plans for a national challenge highlighting student and educator achievements in AI.
  </Step>
  <Step title="K-12 Resource Development">
    Agencies represented on the Task Force seek public-private partnerships with AI industry organizations, academic institutions, and nonprofits to develop online resources teaching foundational AI literacy and critical thinking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Federal Grant Prioritization">
    The Secretary of Education is directed to prioritize AI in discretionary grant programs for teacher training; the Director of the NSF is directed to prioritize research on AI in education.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**[View the Executive Order on the White House website](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/04/advancing-artificial-intelligence-education-for-american-youth/)**

## <Icon icon="flask" size="24" className="inline-block align-text-bottom" /> National Science Foundation

The NSF has been directed to prioritize research on AI in education, including studies on:

- Effectiveness of AI tools in K-12 classrooms
- Equity impacts of AI integration across student populations
- Teacher professional learning models for AI
- AI literacy curriculum design and assessment

## <Icon icon="shield" size="24" className="inline-block align-text-bottom" /> Federal Privacy & Compliance Context

AI tools in K-12 settings must comply with the same federal student-privacy and accessibility laws that govern other educational technology.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="FERPA">
    **Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act**

    Protects the privacy of student education records. AI tools that process student records or generate outputs based on them are subject to FERPA's disclosure, consent, and data-handling requirements.

    Key implications for AI use:
    - Student records may not be shared with AI vendors without consent or a permissible exception (such as the "school official" exception with vendor contract requirements)
    - Districts must maintain direct control over how AI vendors process student data
    - Parents and eligible students retain inspection and amendment rights

    See: [Privacy and Security FAQ](/faq/Privacy and Security)
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="COPPA">
    **Children's Online Privacy Protection Act**

    Applies to AI tools collecting personal information from children under 13. Requires verifiable parental consent before collection, use, or disclosure of children's personal information.

    Key implications for AI use:
    - AI products marketed to or knowingly used by under-13 students need COPPA-compliant consent flows
    - Schools may consent on behalf of parents in narrow educational contexts, but the requirements are specific
    - Vendors must provide notice of data practices

    See: [Under 13 Use FAQ](/faq/Under 13 Use)
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="IDEA & Section 504">
    **Individuals with Disabilities Education Act / Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act**

    AI cannot replace the individualized determinations required for special-education services. Several state and district policies (notably NYCPS) explicitly prohibit using AI to draft IEPs, 504 plans, or eligibility decisions.

    AI tools used to <em>support</em> students with disabilities — assistive technology, accessibility adaptations, translation — must themselves be accessible and aligned with each student's IEP.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## <Icon icon="gavel" size="24" className="inline-block align-text-bottom" /> Federal–State Interaction

Federal AI policy operates alongside — and sometimes in tension with — the [state-level guidance](/resources/aistatepolicies) that 36 states plus Puerto Rico have issued.

<div className="p-4 bg-yellow-50 rounded-md border border-yellow-200">
  <strong>Late-2025 Federal Signals:</strong>
  <ul className="mt-2">
    <li>Federal executive actions in late 2025 signaled interest in limiting state-level AI regulation, raising preemption questions for state K-12 frameworks</li>
    <li>State departments of education are continuing to issue their own guidance under their existing authority over public schools</li>
    <li>Districts navigating both layers should treat state and federal guidance as cumulative, applying the more protective standard where they overlap</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## <Icon icon="graduation-cap" size="24" className="inline-block align-text-bottom" /> What This Means for Educators

Federal policy primarily shapes K-12 AI use through three levers:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Funding" icon="dollar-sign">
    Federal grants increasingly prioritize AI integration and educator AI training, creating financial incentives for districts to adopt aligned policies.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Compliance Floor" icon="shield-check">
    FERPA, COPPA, and IDEA set the legal floor for any AI tool used with students — irrespective of state or district policy.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Research & Resources" icon="flask">
    NSF-funded research and federally-developed resources (curricula, training materials) are flowing to states and districts through public-private partnerships.
  </Card>
  <Card title="National Tone" icon="bullhorn">
    Federal executive actions set the tone for state and district decision-makers, shaping public discourse and accelerating policy development across jurisdictions.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
